We have developed a series of generative language models, collectively named ALIF الف, specifically pretrained on our large-scale ALIF-Urdu-Corpus. Our primary focus has been on creating computationally efficient Small Language Models (SLMs) that offer a strong balance between performance and the resources required for training and deployment. The series includes both base pretrained models and instruction-tuned variants.

All models are built upon the Transformer architecture, with a focus on the decoder-only variant. The models are designed to be lightweight and efficient, making them suitable for a wide range of applications, from research to deployment in low-resource environments.

Training Details

Built upon the Alif 1B base model and fine-tuned on a carefully curated instruction dataset. The instruction tuning process enhances the model's ability to understand and follow natural language instructions in Urdu. For more details, visit our Instruction Tuning page.

Access on Hugging Face Try in Chat Interface

Training Methodology

All base models were pretrained using a standard causal language modeling objective on the ALIF-Urdu-Corpus. Training was performed on high-performance GPUs using PyTorch, with careful hyperparameter optimization for each model size. The instruction-tuned variant underwent additional fine-tuning with supervised learning from instruction datasets.
